(iii) Reading of the Twelves Steps (p.15) THE TWELVE STEPS OF RECOVERY AND GROWTH 1. We admitted we had lost our way and needed direction. 2. We took courage in our hands and asked for help. 3. We trusted the Greater Power of live, love and the world around us. 4. We acknowledged our gifts and our strenghts. 5. We tackled our weaknesses and failures. 6. We committed to the journey even through the tough times. 7. We took care and control of our bodies. 8. We learnt to think by reason rather than by feelings and imagination. 9. We developed will power to do the right things in spite of our feelings. 10. We took our responsible and caring place in society. 11. We grew daily closer to maturity. 12. We carried Grow’s message to others in need. (iv) The Grow Commitment is recited together (p.16)

GROW’S COMMITMENT TO CONFIDENTIALITY The leader reads: The first three clauses apply to everyone present. To be read or respected by all: • I will respect the confidential nature of what is disclosed at Grow meetings. • I promise never to lead or aid a member of Grow in any serious wrong, • What I contribute at Grow meetings will be the truth. The leader reads: The next clause applies to those who are ready. • I commit myself to a journey of personal growth and change by using The Grow Program and the Group Method. The leader reads the following legal requirement: While Grow guarantees confidentiality in the working of it’s groups, there are three areas when by law the group cannot guarantee this strict confidentiality, namely: 1. Where harm is threatened on self or others. 2. Where a minor is at risk of abuse. 3. Where serious crime in involved. To be read by all: In Grow we believe in one another, we love one another and we trust one another.

2. First Group Interaction (30-35 minutes) Note: For newcomers give a 1-2 minute explanation of Grow: THE JOURNEY – FOR BEGINNING GROWERS What is Grow? Grow is a mutual help movement working in the field of mental health. Grow wishes to make a connection with everybody – those of all religions and those of none. Essential to Grow’s message is that we reach out and connect with something beyond ourselves. Within this text the word ‘God’ is used sparingly. The hope is that the term ‘Greater Power’ is more accommodating – to those of all religions and those of none. Our meetings are anonymous, non-denominational and open to all. Since the groups are run by our own members, quality leadership training is provided. Groups are small, usually in single figures and last about two hours. They are followed by refreshements. Our meetings are free with small voluntary contributions welcome to cover the venue expenses. No introductions are needed. Just come along. Con Keogh, with a small group of other people, started Grow in Sydney, Austrailia in 1957, as an offshot of Alcoholics Anonymous. It has since spread to other countries coming to Ireland in 1969. The Grow 12 Step Program was born out of the pain and wisdom of people grappling with recovery from various forms of mental illness. From there it quickly evolved into a movement which included personal growth and prevenion as well as recovery. Middle Routine (20-30 minutes) Note: The group is urged to spot and discourage any turning of the discussion back to personal problems. (i) Now we pass to our Affirmation of Good (p.100) and the Declarations of Trust (p.101) – both are voluntary ad profoundly personal and therefore need prior reflection. THE AFFIRMATION OF GOOD We believe that our lives up to now have een neither wholly good nor wholly bad. We believe that we are capable of change and growth. We Acknowledge and ask pardon for the wrongs we have done and for the needed good that we failed to do. We freely forgive from our hearts those who have wronged us or failed us – including our own selves. We hope for ourselves and wish to one another and to every human being, enduring friendships, and end to hostility, and the best in life, love and happiness. DECLARATIONS OF TRUST The ethos of Grow is about letting go, about accepting that we need help from others. About accepting there are things outside our control that can help us if we allow them to. I am capable of being loved, loving myself and loving others. I am a connecting link and I have a unique part to play in this world. I accept that my own efforts for self-transformation are irreplaceable. I commit to practicing patience and perseverence in following The Grow Program. We as a group will be concerned not just for ourselve sbut for one another. We commit to being part of a Greater Power, either spiritual or practical, that enables us to reach towards living a freer and better life.
